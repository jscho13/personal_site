    How to Become More Frugal In Your Everyday Life

    February 12, 2017
    in Savings, Budgeting

    A lot of us know that being able to keep the wallet closed on impulsive buys is important to saving money. But changing our habits to match that feeling can be difficult. When it comes to changing how I act, no amount of tips and techniques really sticks to me until I change how I think about something. Going to the gym regularly was one of those life habits that I knew I should do, but had trouble actually committing to! It wasn’t until I read the book Spark: The Revolutionary New Science of Exercise and the Brain by John Ratey that I really began to appreciate just how amazing exercise is for your mental health. Once I understood that exercise wasn’t a chore, but another technique to rejuvenate myself I became far more motivated to change myself as a person.

    Being a smart spender is no exception. Understanding why I’m spending too much was key for me to change my habits. I believe a lot of us today are trapped in the consumer mindset.

    Ways We’re Geared Towards a Consumer Mindset

    In a nutshell, the spending mindset is another way to describe that we spend more money on things we don’t need. Take the materialistic value of a watch for example.

    At the end of the day watches are supposed to be a tool that helps us keep track of time. However, we’ve turned watches into something that represents more than it’s original purpose. Instead of being a tool, watches have turned into a status symbol to represent wealth and fashion tastes. If we really wanted to just check the time we could always check our phones. Good brand marketing is designed to pressure us into buying something nicer than we need! It’s a classic example of a consumer mindset.

    Societal values is another way that consumer mindsets manifest themselves.
